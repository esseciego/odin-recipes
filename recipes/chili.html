Vegan Chili

Adapted from Nisha's "The Best Vegan Chili" recipeon Rainbow Plant Life

Description

Spicy, hearty, and packed with flavor.

Ingredients

Chili
  • 3 tbp olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 6 garlic cloves, chopped fine
  • 2 jalapenos, diced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 4 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tbsp oregano
  • 2 red bell peppers, diced
  • 5 medium-sized carrots, diced
  • 2 cans of pinto beans, drained
  • 1 can of navy beans, drained
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cans of diced, fire-roasted tomatoes
  • 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt + more as needed
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar
Toppings of choice
  • Diced avocado
  • Sliced green onions or chopped cilantro
  • Shredded vegan cheese

Steps

  1. Heat saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil, and once shimmering, add onions and season with a few pinches of salt. Stir frequently and cook onions until golden brown, stirring occasionally, about 10 minutes. If they start to burn, stir more frequently and/or add a splash of water.
  2. Add garlic and jalapenos, and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently.
  3. Add carrot and cook for 5 minutes. Then add peppers and cook for 3 minutes.
  4. Stir in chili powder, cumin, paprika, and oregano and stir vigorously for 30 minutes.
  5. Pour in pinto beans, navy, beans, bay leaves, soy sauce, diced tomatoes, salt, black pepper to taste, and 1 tbsp maple syrup. Stir well.
  6. Bring chili to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cook, stirring every 10 minutes, until thick and velvety and flavors have melded together, about 1 hour.
  7. Stir in in vinegar. Taste and add up to 1 more tbsp maple syrup for sweetness to balance any bitterness. Season with salt and pepper, as needed.
  8. Serve with toppings of choice.
